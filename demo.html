ALL ABOUT FIBRE

CHECK COVERAGE IN YOUR AREA

First we had dial-up, then came ADSL, today fibre optic cables are rolling out across South Africa and everyone wants in. Simply put, fibre is currently the fastest way to connect to the internet.

Fibre cables consist of bundles of glass threads which use light waves to transmit data at incredible speeds – about 100 times faster than your current ADSL connection on a 100Mbps line. Not only is the transfer incredibly fast, but it is far more stable, so the quality of the transfer is not affected by the distance between your home and the exchange as happens with the copper wires used for normal ADSL connections.

The increased speed has obvious advantages – particularly if you enjoy watching videos, use streaming to watch your favourite TV series or movies, or are a serious gamer. Fibre’s bigger bandwidth (the rate at which data is transferred) is also much better at handling more than one person connecting to the internet than a copper-wire ADSL connection. That means no more arguments about who gets to connect first, and no more “waiting patiently” for a few hours while the movie you want to watch downloads. With connections of 25 Mbps (link to Flix25), streaming via Netflix or ShowMax is pure pleasure.
